# Authentication User Guide

## Getting Started with Your Account

Welcome to the MCP platform! This guide will help you understand and use all the authentication features available to keep your account secure while providing a seamless user experience.

## Account Registration

### Creating Your Account

1. **Visit the Sign Up Page**: Go to [app.mcp-platform.com/register](https://app.mcp-platform.com/register)
2. **Choose Your Registration Method**:
   - **Email Registration**: Sign up with your email address
   - **Social Login**: Quick signup with Google, Microsoft, or GitHub

### Email Registration Process

1. **Enter Your Email**: Use your work or personal email address
2. **Create a Strong Password**:
   - At least 12 characters long
   - Contains uppercase and lowercase letters
   - Includes numbers and special characters (!@#$%^&*)
   - Example: `SecurePass!2024`
3. **Confirm Your Password**: Re-enter your password to verify
4. **Agree to Terms**: Review and accept the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
5. **Verify Your Email**: Check your inbox for a verification email and click the confirmation link

### Social Login Benefits

- **Faster Registration**: No password creation required
- **Existing Credentials**: Use your existing Google, Microsoft, or GitHub account
- **One-Click Access**: Quick login with saved preferences

## Logging In

### Standard Login

1. **Go to Login Page**: Visit [app.mcp-platform.com/login](https://app.mcp-platform.com/login)
2. **Enter Your Credentials**:
   - Email address used for registration
   - Your password
3. **Click "Sign In"**: Access your secure dashboard

### Social Login

1. **Choose Your Provider**: Click the Google, Microsoft, or GitHub button
2. **Authorize Access**: Grant permission to access your basic profile information
3. **Complete Login**: You'll be redirected back to your dashboard

### Session Management

- **Remember Me**: Check this box to stay logged in for 30 days
- **Private Browsing**: Use incognito mode for temporary sessions
- **Multiple Devices**: Stay logged in across all your devices

## Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

### Why Use 2FA?

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security to protect your account from unauthorized access. Even if someone steals your password, they won't be able to access your account without your second factor.

### Setting Up 2FA

1. **Go to Security Settings**: Navigate to Settings → Security
2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication**
3. **Download Your Backup Codes**: Save these securely for account recovery
4. **Set Up Authentication App**:
   - Install Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator
   - Scan the QR code provided
   - Enter the 6-digit code to verify setup

### Using 2FA

When logging in from a new device:
1. **Enter Email and Password**: Complete the first authentication step
2. **Enter 2FA Code**: Open your authentication app and enter the 6-digit code
3. **Complete Login**: Access your account with enhanced security

### Backup Codes

If you lose access to your authentication app:
1. **Use Backup Code**: Enter one of your saved 8-character backup codes
2. **Generate New Codes**: After using a backup code, generate new ones
3. **Keep Codes Secure**: Store them in a safe place like a password manager

## Password Management

### Changing Your Password

1. **Access Account Settings**: Go to Settings → Account
2. **Click "Change Password"**
3. **Enter Current Password**: Verify your identity
4. **Create New Password**: Follow the security requirements
5. **Confirm New Password**: Re-enter your new password
6. **Update Password**: Save your changes

### Password Requirements

- **Length**: At least 12 characters
- **Complexity**: Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters
- **Security**: Not commonly used or easily guessable
- **Unique**: Different from passwords used on other sites

### Resetting Your Password

If you forget your password:
1. **Click "Forgot Password"**: On the login page
2. **Enter Your Email**: Provide the email associated with your account
3. **Check Your Email**: Look for password reset instructions
4. **Create New Password**: Follow the link and set a new password
5. **Secure Your Account**: Your new password is now active

## Single Sign-On (SSO)

### What is SSO?

Single Sign-On lets you access multiple applications with one login. If your organization uses SSO, you can log in once and access all connected services without re-entering your credentials.

### SSO Benefits

- **Time Savings**: No need to remember multiple passwords
- **Enhanced Security**: Centralized authentication and access control
- **Consistent Experience**: Same login process across all applications
- **Administrative Control**: IT manages access and security policies

### Using SSO

If your organization has SSO enabled:
1. **Company Login Page**: Use your company's login portal
2. **One-Click Access**: Automatically logged in to all connected services
3. **Seamless Integration**: Switch between applications without re-authenticating

## Troubleshooting

### Common Login Issues

**Can't Remember Password?**
- Use the "Forgot Password" link
- Check your spam folder for reset emails
- Contact support if you don't receive reset instructions

**2FA Problems?**
- Use backup codes if you lose access to your authentication app
- Contact support for account recovery
- Verify your authentication app time settings

**Account Locked?**
- Wait 30 minutes for automatic unlock
- Contact support if you believe the lock is in error
- Ensure you're using correct credentials

## Best Practices for Account Security

### Password Security

- **Use Unique Passwords**: Don't reuse passwords from other services
- **Password Manager**: Consider using a password manager for security
- **Regular Updates**: Change your password every 90 days
- **Avoid Personal Info**: Don't use personal information in passwords

### Two-Factor Authentication

- **Always Enable 2FA**: Add this extra security layer to your account
- **Secure Backup Codes**: Save backup codes in a secure location
- **Multiple Authenticators**: Consider having multiple 2FA methods available
- **Regular Updates**: Keep your authentication app updated

### Session Management

- **Log Out Public Devices**: Always log out from shared computers
- **Monitor Active Sessions**: Regularly review where you're logged in
- **Use Private Browsing**: Use incognito mode on public computers
- **Stay Updated**: Keep your browser and apps updated for security
